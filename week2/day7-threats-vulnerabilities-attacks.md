## Focus
Understanding common cybersecurity threats, vulnerabilities, and attack methods, and how defenders identify and mitigate them.

---

## Topics Covered
- Difference between threats, vulnerabilities, and risks
- Common malware types (virus, worm, trojan, ransomware)
- Social engineering and phishing attacks
- Insider threats and credential abuse
- How attackers exploit misconfigurations
- Importance of patching and system updates

---

## Key Concepts
- **Threat:** Anything capable of causing harm
- **Vulnerability:** A weakness that can be exploited
- **Risk:** Likelihood and impact of a threat exploiting a vulnerability

---

## Real-World Examples
- Phishing emails leading to credential compromise
- Unpatched systems exploited by malware
- Misconfigured permissions exposing sensitive data
- Weak passwords enabling unauthorized access

---

## Defensive Practices
- Applying system and software updates regularly
- Using least privilege access controls
- Monitoring logs for suspicious activity
- User awareness and security training
- Implementing basic network segmentation

---

## Tools & Techniques Referenced
- Antivirus and endpoint protection
- Patch management systems
- Log monitoring tools
- Intrusion detection concepts
- Security awareness training

---

## Key Takeaways
Security threats are often successful due to human error, misconfigurations, or unpatched systems. Understanding how attacks occur is essential for building effective defenses and responding to incidents.
